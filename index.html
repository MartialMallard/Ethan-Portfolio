    Hi! I'm Ethan.

    Projects and Experience

    Realm of the Mad God

    Level Design - Enemy Design - Item Design

    Realm of the Mad God is a free-to-play roguelike MMORPG with bullet hell combat and permadeath.

    - Participated as a volunteer Level Designer in the game's UGC (User-Generated Content) Program

    - Project Lead for an endgame-difficulty level built from initial concept to production

    - Worked alongside the official design team to add enemies and bosses to upcoming releases

    Wynncraft

    System Design - System Balance

    Wynncraft is an MMORPG game built entirely in Minecraft, and one of the most popular multiplayer servers in the game.

    - Worked on designing for and reworking various playable classes

    - Designed and balanced the progression of multiple class skill trees for several archetypes

    Chew Chew Train

    System Design - Level Design - Game Programming

    Chew Chew Train is a roguelike management game developed within 7 days for Mashup Game Jam 2025. The theme of the jam was "Transportation".

    - Designed and programmed a custom event system, allowing unique NPC interactions

    - Designed and programmed the game's fully randomized map generation

    - Designed and implemented most of the game's user interface

    Slime Lives

    System Design - Level Design - Game Programming - Art

    Slime Lives is a 2D Puzzle Platformer that was developed within 5 days for Jamsepticeye, theme of the jam was "Death is an Opportunity".

    - Created 15 unique levels across 3 worlds

    - Designed 3 player abilities that change the ways you interact with death

    - Assisted with programming and art

    About Me

    My Journey

    I had originally started my close involvement with Realm of the Mad God back in 2021, where during a time of personal struggle I wrote and published a game design essay to the community's subreddit that detailed some of the most prevalent issues facing the game. This was met with a large amount of community discussion and even led to recognition by the official design team at that time, which springboarded towards being part of the first wave of closed testers that weren't strictly invited through personal recommendation.

    During the following year, I spent my time independently learning through the remaining documentation and updating it myself
