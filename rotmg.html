Realm of the Mad God Exalt

Realm of the Mad God is a co-op, top-down 2D, rogue-like bullet hell MMORPG with permadeath.

What I Accomplished:

- Developed a full-length endgame difficulty level from scratch as part of the User-Generated Content program, including multiple new bosses, enemies, and items.

- Worked with the design team to provide additional smaller content to major updates.

- Trained new members of the User-Generated content team to be more familiar with the available scripting tools and how to design content.

- Provided specs for new scripting functions that were added to the available tools used by the official design team.

- Participated in discussions with the design team to plan for future content updates.

- Contributed detailed art mockups to the art team that were used as reference for final products.

- Participated in weekly closed testing sessions as part of the Closed Testing program, providing valuable feedback to the design team.

Additional Details
My Journey

I had originally started my close involvement with Realm of the Mad God back in 2021, where during a time of personal struggle I wrote and published a game design essay to the community's subreddit that detailed some of the most prevalent issues facing the game. This was met with a large amount of community discussion and even led to recognition by the official design team at that time, which springboarded towards being part of the first wave of closed testers that weren't strictly invited through personal recommendation.

The opportunity provided to me through this experience wasn't without struggle, however. I was thrust into an environment where many veterans of both the Closed Testing and User-Generated Content programs had left the team, with the only thing left behind for me to work with was outdated documentation. At that point, it became evident that I would have to be the person to take the torch if the program were to survive.

During the following year, I spent my time independently learning through the remaining documentation and updating it myself. It was only towards the tail-end of this period that the program started to recover, as new faces decided to stick around longer and were more willing to interact with the existing documentation. Through my effort of consistent activity, I was able to provide a foundation for the community of User-Generated Content members to recover.

My biggest accomplishment working on Realm of the Mad God is my full-length endgame dungeon project, the Spectral Penitentiary.
